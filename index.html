About

We invest the way we operate — directly.

Cascade Operators Group is a three-person partnership — an operator, an investor, and a chief financial officer — bringing the experience of much larger firms to a single, hands-on acquisition in the Pacific Northwest.

We are principals, not a fund. We invest our own capital, take operational responsibility for what we buy, and intend to hold and improve it for the long term.

Our goal is not simply to buy a company. It is to carry forward what has already been built — with fresh energy, modern systems, and the steady attention of owners who plan to be there.

The partnership

Operating, finance, and capital under one roof.

Benjamin Cassiday

Operator

Ben has spent more than a decade running day-to-day operations across consumer goods, healthcare services, and technology-driven businesses. As a co-founder and operator, he helped scale one company from startup to $13M in annual revenue within 24 months, building manufacturing, customer service, market education, and distributed teams across the United States and the Philippines.

He specializes in the thoughtful use of technology, expanded marketing, and modernized operating practices that help mature businesses improve without losing what made them successful.

Morris “Moshe” Shemian

Commercial Transactions

Hank Hague

What we look for

A clear profile, deliberately drawn.

Our criteria are tight because our intention is clear. The profile below is meant to make it easy for owners, brokers, and advisors to recognize when we are the right call — and when we are not.

Geography

Pacific Northwest

Within roughly 50 miles of Portland, Oregon or Sequim, Washington.

Situation

Owner-led transitions

Retirement, succession planning, or estates where the next steward has not yet been identified.

Industries

Service, manufacturing, distribution, retirement services

Established operations with durable customer relationships. Not retail. Not franchises.

Posture

Principals, not a fund

We invest our own capital, take operational responsibility, and intend to hold and run what we buy for the long term.
